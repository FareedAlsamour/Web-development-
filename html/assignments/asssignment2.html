Just Answering A Video Call Could Compromise Your WhatsApp Account Important points

This sounds filmy, but Google Project Zero security researcher Natalie Silvanovich found a critical vulnerability in WhatsApp messenger that could have allowed hackers to remotely take full control of your WhatsApp just by video calling you over the messaging app.		 Critical vulnerability found in WhatsApp messenger

The vulnerability is a memory heap overflow issue which is triggered when a user receives a specially crafted malformed RTP packet via a video call request, which results in the corruption error and crashing the WhatsApp mobile app.		 The flaw affects Android and iOS apps

Since the vulnerability affect RTP (Real-time Transport Protocol) implementation of Whatsapp, the flaw affects Android and iOS apps, but not WhatsApp Web that relies on WebRTC for video calls.		 You should update your WhatsApp as soon as possible

It isn't the first time

Two months ago, researchers also discovered a flaw in the way WhatsApp mobile app connects with WhatsApp Web that
allowed malicious users to intercept and modify the content of messages sent in both private as well as group conversations.
